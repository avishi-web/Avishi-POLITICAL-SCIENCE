<h1>Ideology and Objectives of Political Parties</h1>

<p>
<h3>1. Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP):</h3>
- Ideology: The BJP is a right-wing political party with a nationalist and Hindutva ideology. It advocates for cultural nationalism, economic liberalism, and a strong emphasis on national security.
<br>
- Objectives: In Arunachal Pradesh, the BJP aims to promote its ideology while focusing on development initiatives, infrastructure projects, and good governance. It seeks to expand its presence in the state and establish itself as a credible alternative to other political parties.
<br>

<h3>2. Indian National Congress (INC):</h3>
- Ideology: The INC is a centrist to center-left political party in India. Historically, it has stood for secularism, social justice, and inclusive economic growth.
<br>
- Objectives: In Arunachal Pradesh, the INC aims to uphold its secular and inclusive ideology while focusing on welfare programs, education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. It seeks to regain political power and address the needs of the state's diverse population.
<br>

<h3>3. People's Party of Arunachal (PPA):</h3>
- Ideology: The PPA is a regional political party in Arunachal Pradesh. It primarily focuses on the interests of the state and its people, advocating for regional autonomy and the preservation of indigenous cultures.
<br>
- Objectives: The PPA aims to safeguard the rights of indigenous communities, promote regional development, and address local issues such as land rights, environmental conservation, and infrastructure development. It seeks to assert Arunachal Pradesh's autonomy within the federal structure of India.
<br>

<h3>4. National People's Party (NPP):</h3>
- Ideology: The NPP is a center-right political party in India. It emphasizes regional empowerment, good governance, and development-oriented policies.
<br>
- Objectives: In Arunachal Pradesh, the NPP aims to address the aspirations of the people by focusing on development, transparency, and accountability in governance. It seeks to provide an alternative to traditional political parties and promote a more inclusive and responsive government.
<br>

<h3>5. NCP (Nationalist Congress Party):</h3>
- Ideology: The NCP is a centrist political party in India. It advocates for secularism, social justice, and federalism.
<br>
- Objectives: In Arunachal Pradesh, the NCP aims to promote its ideology while focusing on issues such as farmers' welfare, employment generation, and equitable development. It seeks to strengthen federalism and ensure that the concerns of smaller states like Arunachal Pradesh are adequately addressed at the national level.
<br>

<h3>6. JDU (Janata Dal United):</h3>
- Ideology: The JDU is a center-left political party in India. It espouses secularism, social justice, and inclusive development.
<br>
- Objectives: In Arunachal Pradesh, the JDU aims to promote its ideology while focusing on issues such as education, healthcare, infrastructure, and rural development. It seeks to collaborate with like-minded parties to address the needs of the state's population and contribute to its overall progress.
</p>
